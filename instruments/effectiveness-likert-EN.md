# Effectiveness Likert questionnaire — English version

Questionnaire designed *ad hoc* by the research team to assess the guardians' perception of the effectiveness of the Torddis system. It was administered after the 30-minute pilot session.

Each statement is rated on a 5-point Likert scale: **1 = strongly disagree**, **5 = strongly agree**.

## Statements

| # | Statement | Scale |
|---|-----------|-------|
| 1 | The device was perceived by the student as appealing. | 1–5 |
| 2 | The mobile application accurately recorded the events that occurred. | 1–5 |
| 3 | The student did not perceive the presence of the device as intrusive. | 1–5 |
| 4 | The device was used throughout the effective time devoted to school tasks. | 1–5 |
| 5 | After discontinuing use of the device, the student demonstrated improvement in self-regulation and concentration while completing homework at home. | 1–5 |
| 6 | Although the device usage time was short, it is expected to contribute significantly to task completion and to the academic performance of the student. | 1–5 |
| 7 | You would be willing to continue using the device. | 1–5 |

## Statistical analysis

See the notebooks `01-normality-tests.ipynb`, `02-pearson-correlation.ipynb`, `03-anova-analysis.ipynb`, `04-post-hoc-tests.ipynb`, `05-spearman-correlation.ipynb` and `06-kruskal-wallis.ipynb` in the `../analysis/` folder for the statistical procedure applied to the responses.

## Notes

- The guardians answered the questionnaire in the presence of the researcher, who was available to clarify doubts without biasing the answer.
- Item 5 (improvement in self-regulation) requires an *ex-post* judgement from the guardian and was therefore explained in detail before the answer.
- No test–retest or formal construct-validity study was carried out within the scope of this pilot study.
