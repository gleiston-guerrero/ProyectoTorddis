## Informed consent to participate in the study

**Project title:** Torddis --- Real-time IoT and Artificial Intelligence system to support at-home learning and mitigate parental absenteeism.

**Principal investigator at the study site:** Gleiston Cicerón Guerrero Ulloa, Ph.D. (Faculty of Engineering Sciences, Universidad Técnica Estatal de Quevedo). E-mail: gguerrero@uteq.edu.ec.

**Committee that approved the study:** Research Committee of the Faculty of Engineering Sciences of the Universidad Técnica Estatal de Quevedo (UTEQ).

---

### 1. Purpose of the study

The purpose of the study is to evaluate the usability and effectiveness of the Torddis system, which uses a smart camera and a mobile application to monitor distraction parameters in school-aged children while they perform homework at home.

### 2. Procedure

If you agree to participate, you will be asked to:

- Install the Torddis mobile application on your smartphone.
- Place the IoT device on your child's study desk.
- Allow the system to operate for a session of approximately 30 minutes while the child does homework.
- Answer a usability questionnaire (SUS), a brief demographic survey, a Likert effectiveness questionnaire and eight open-ended questions.

The total session, including set-up and questionnaires, should not exceed 60 minutes.

### 3. Data that will be collected

From the Torddis system:

- Video frames of the study area (processed in real time and not stored).
- Derived events (presence, facial expression, drowsiness, use of non-permitted objects) with time-stamps.

From you:

- Responses to the SUS, demographic, Likert and open-ended questionnaires.

From the child: **no** information is collected directly. Only the events derived from the monitoring.

### 4. Voluntary participation

Participation is voluntary. You may withdraw at any time, including during or after the session, without any consequence for you or your family. You may request the deletion of all data collected up to that moment without needing to justify.

### 5. Confidentiality and data protection

- No names, surnames, identification documents, addresses or phone numbers will be requested.
- Your participation will be coded with a pseudonymous identifier (e.g. `G01`).
- Data will be stored encrypted (AES-256) on servers controlled by the research team.
- Only authorised researchers will have access to the data.
- The pseudonymised data may be published in an open scientific repository (Zenodo) under a Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 licence. In no case will images, video or audio be published.

### 6. Benefits and risks

**Benefits:** you contribute to the improvement of a technological educational system that could benefit other families. No financial compensation is offered.

**Risks:** the risks are minimal. The child may initially feel observed; the system is designed to reduce this perception (see item 3 of the effectiveness questionnaire). The session can be stopped at any time.

### 7. Contact

For questions, doubts or withdrawal from the study, please contact us at: gguerrero@uteq.edu.ec.

If you have ethical concerns that were not resolved with the researchers, you may directly contact the Research Committee of the Faculty of Engineering Sciences of the Universidad Técnica Estatal de Quevedo (UTEQ) through the Software Engineering Programme: carrerasoftware@uteq.edu.ec.
